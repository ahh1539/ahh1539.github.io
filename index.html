2205 BCE
Xia dynasty founded
Scholars still argue if this dynasty existed as there is not contemporary records
1600 BCE
Shang Dynasty Founded
A small society was made with a hierarchy of power. Thought to have 5 successive capitals. The Shang king acted as a priest and ritual leader.
1250 BCE
Lady Haos tomb constructed
Thought to be of royal family, buried with 16 human sacrafices and bronze vessels
1200 BCE
Chariots came into use
Chariots were typically pulled by two or four horses and used in warfare
1050 BCE
Zhou Dynasty
Zhou defeated the Shang and established a new dynasty
722 BCE
Spring and Autum period
This marks the first half of Zhou dynasty rule
551 BCE
Birth of Confucius
Born in the state of Lu. Became Chinas first moral philosipher. Gave rise to Confucianism
403 BCE
Warring states period
Chinese states fought and stuggled for dominance. The Zhou king at the time was merely a figurehead. The dominating state was the Qin.
400 BCE
Legalism became form of governing
Legalism rose because of the need to govern people in all the new land and territory being captured in war.
370 BCE
Mencius born
Mencius was another philosopher who followed a modified version of Confucian teachings. Believed that humans were naturally good.
221 BCE
Qin Dynasty
Qin Dynasty was founded after Qin defeated all the remaining states during the warring period. Had the first emperor or Huangdi. Know for his cruelty and being an megalomaniac ruler
206 BCE
Han Dynasty
Han dynasty is formed after some conflict where Liu Bang eventually came out on top. The Han dynasty leaned away from legalist ways and more towards Confucianist policies.
199 BCE
Han monopolies established
Han ruled monopolies were established to produce salt, iron, and liquor. They generated much of the governments funds.
130 BCE
Silk road begins
The silk road was a trading passage between china and the westward european nations. It carried goods and ideas back and forth
25
Eastern Han
Liu Xiu defeated all his opponents and overthrew Wang Mang, and reestablished the Han dynasty
104
Paper & Books invented
Paper and books were invented by putting together bark, bamboo, hemp and other ingredients
184
Rebellion of Great way of Peace
A large rebellion was started by a cult called The Great Way of Peace and in 189 a warlord gained control of the capital and made the emperor his pawn
220
The Three Kingdoms
The three kingdoms Shu, Wu, and Wei all fought for power. Many great battles were fought during this time among the three generals: Cao cao, Sun Quan, and Liu Pei.
280
Jin Dynasty
Jin dynasty official established after the Wu were defeated in a naval campaign. China was once again unified
304
Sixteen Kingdoms
Nomadic peoples had an uprising and sacked the capital, and many warlords fought for control once again. As a result four short lived dynasties were made.
350
Buddhist monk Kumarajiva settled in China
Central Asian Buddhist monk Kumarajiva settled in China and directed several thousand monks in the translation of 38 Buddhist texts. This was crucial in the spread of Buddhism in China
581
Sui Dynasty
Sui dynasty reunited china be unifying the northern and southern dynasties. Sui was very short lived, and there was only two Sui emperors.
618
Tang Dynasty
A Rebellion overthrew the Sui. Many important events happened during the Tang, including
  • Opening of Grand Canal
  • Civil service exam
  • Two capitals
653
Tang code of 653
The code of 653 was a governing set of laws with over 500 articles specifying punishments for different crimes
660
Empress Wu takes control
She proclaimed herself the emperor of the tang and ruled for quite some time. She was a stern ruler. During her long reign totaling 50 years, the empire became relatively stable, peaceful, powerful, and prosperous.
960
Song Dynasty
Southern migration increased significantly. New advances in industry and agriculture drove economic growth. Civil service exam dominated the elites lifestyle. Founded by Zhao Kuangyin, a tactical genius
1120
Paper Money Officially used by Song Dynasty
Paper money started being used officially by the Song government. Started in order to reduce bulk of coins weight during large transactions.
1162
Birth of Genghis Khan
Genghis Khan was born. He became a famous Mongol leader and general that lead the Mongols to rule over China and defeat the Jin. Named great khan in 1206
1275
Marco Polo Arrives in China
Marco Polo was an Italian merchant, who traveled to Mongol ruled China and served Genghis khan. He dtayed in China for 20 years.
1276
Yuan Dynasty
Mongol rule begins with the Yuan dynasty after defeating the Jin (Jurchen ruled dynasty)
1368
Ming Dynasty
Founded by peasant turned general Zhu Yuanzhang who was an able warrior and a commanding leader
1405
Great Chinese Voyages
Zheng He an imperial eunuch lead a massive voyage that brought them to many different civilizations where they would show off the might of the Chinese empire
1420
Forbidden City Palace Opened
The forbidden city was opened in Beijing, this is where many emperors would rule from and is the center of the city of Beijing. The entire city was built like a rectangle around the palace.
1638
Qing Dynasty
Qing Dynasty founded and ruled by Manchus after the collapse of the Ming dynasty, initially met by some resistance but eventually prevailed. This would be China's last dynasty.
1645
Mandatory hairstyle
Decree made by Manchu leaders that all men must wear a queue hairstyle will be executed within 10 days
1750
Xinjian Province added to China
The Xinjiang province captured by Qing after defeating the Mongols and Uighurs populated there. The province was named Xinjiang and was added to China.
1840
Opium wars
Sixteen British warships were sent to China and they successfully defeated the forces stationed there and forced the ports to open for trade. This marked the beginning of the Opium wars and forced china to be open to the rest of the world.
1842
Treaty of Nanjing
The Chinese forces after being defeated many times were forced to sign a peace treaty ending the opium wars and giving the British favorable trade tariffs
